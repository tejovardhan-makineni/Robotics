# 24-Week Robotics Mastery Schedule

This is a serious but realistic schedule. If you move faster, do not skip projects. Projects are where the knowledge settles.

## Weeks 1-4: Foundations

Notebooks:
- 1 Basics
- 2 Kinematics
- 3 Dynamics and control
- 11 Math
- 12 Spatial math

Deliverable:
- A written explanation of frames, feedback, and state/action/observation.
- A working 2-link arm controller.

## Weeks 5-8: Estimation, Mapping, Planning

Notebooks:
- 4 State estimation
- 5 Mapping and SLAM intro
- 6 Motion planning
- 14 Trajectory generation
- 16 Mobile robot models

Deliverable:
- A simulated mobile robot that estimates state and follows a planned path.

## Weeks 9-12: Manipulation And Vision

Notebooks:
- 13 Dynamics
- 17 Jacobian control
- 18 Vision geometry
- 19 Lidar and ICP
- 20 Grasping

Deliverable:
- A simulated arm that reaches objects using camera geometry or known object coordinates.

## Weeks 13-16: Robot Software

Notebooks:
- 15 LQR and MPC
- 21 ROS 2 architecture
- 22 Simulation and domain randomization
- 27 Safety
- 28 Multi-robot coordination

Deliverable:
- A ROS-style architecture diagram and a simulation with logs, safety checks, and evaluation metrics.

## Weeks 17-20: Robot Learning

Notebooks:
- 7 Imitation learning
- 8 Reinforcement learning
- 23 Dataset engineering
- 24 Behavior cloning MLP
- 25 Diffusion policy intuition
- 26 Continuous-control RL

Deliverable:
- A dataset card, trained behavior cloning policy, and closed-loop evaluation report.

## Weeks 21-24: Frontier And Capstone

Notebooks:
- 9 Modern robot learning and VLAs
- 10 Capstone robot stack
- 29 Reading robotics research
- 30 Master capstone portfolio

Deliverable:
- A final portfolio project with a short report, demo, safety plan, and connection to at least one current robotics paper.

## Weekly Rhythm

Each week:
- 2 notebook sessions
- 1 implementation session
- 1 reading session
- 1 written reflection

Written reflection prompts:
- What broke?
- What assumption was wrong?
- What would change on real hardware?
- What metric would prove improvement?
